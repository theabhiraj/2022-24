OUR HISTORY

Coffee was introduced to Istanbul and quickly became a popular beverage. Coffee shops, or "qahveh khaneh" in Turkish, were established as social hubs where people could gather, socialize, and enjoy coffee together.

Coffee shops soon spread throughout Europe and the rest of the world. In the 17th and 18th centuries, coffee houses in London played a key role in the development of political and cultural movements.

In the United States, coffee shops first appeared in the 17th century, but it was not until the 20th century that they became popular gathering places for people of all walks of life.
